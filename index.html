This project aims to investigate whether Tom Brady is the GOAT (greatest of all time), using a data driven approach. Over the course of NFL history, there have been many excellent players to come and go. Yet, none of them have been deemed the title of ‚ÄòGOAT‚Äô (Greatest Of All Time), that is, until Tom Brady. As a lifelong Tom Brady and Patriots fan, I wanted to investigate the data behind this claim - what makes Tom Brady the GOAT and does the data back it up? In order to analyze Tom Brady's performance, I used a dataset from FiveThirtyEight which included statistics from every single NFL game from 1920 to today. Some of the relevant information the dataset held was home and away teams playing, their starting Quarterbacks, projected rankings of Quarterbacks, and post game rankings. To rank players over such a long period of time, FiveThirtyEight uses Elo ratings, which is an algorithm for ranking players in competitive games. Teams and quarterbacks with higher Elo ratings have a higher probability of winning than teams and quarterbacks with lower Elo ratings. After games, Elo ratings are updated, and if a team with a lower Elo rating beats a team with a higher Elo rating, their rating increases a larger amount than the winning team would if the winning team had a higher Elo rating to begin with. While its predictions may not be as accurate as lines in Las Vegas, they are constantly updating and adapting to factors like home team advantage and margin of victory. When FiveThirtyEight uses Elo ratings for the NFL, the Elo ratings also have some level of mapping to points scored in games, but the math behind that is not necessary for this analysis. Now enough of this technical jargon, let‚Äôs get into the data!

The first line of analysis I conducted analyzed Tom Brady‚Äôs performance over time. I wanted to see his numbers and stats, and how he had performed over the lifetime of his career. Using the data provided, I isolated to games where Tom Brady was the home quarterback, and selected his post game Elo rating. The graph below depicts his performance over his time on the New England Patriots. While his Elo rating started off low when he took over for the injured Drew Bledsoe (previous starting quarterback in 2001), it spiked up in 2002 and 2004, when he won the Super Bowl Championship. His performance stayed fairly consistent as he claimed the title of national champion again in 2005. While he did not win another Super Bowl until 2015, his performance continued to rise, and he was named NFL MVP in 2007. In his last three super bowl wins (2015, 2017, 2019), his Elo ratings stayed relatively high, as he showed profound dominance in his position. Even as he reached his 40s, when many deemed him too old to play, he became the oldest NFL quarterback to win a super bowl.

Next, I wanted to examine whether Brady‚Äôs performance differed whether he was playing a home versus away game. I expected the results to show his home performance to be higher, as he would be more comfortable in his own stadium with his own fans. The data, however, told a different story. While the ratings closely tracked each other, Tom Brady actually had a higher average Elo rating for away games compared to home ones, 191.7 and 183.97 respectively. One possible explanation for this could be that over the long course of his career, he gained comfort in other stadiums and there was not a significant difference to him. In addition, it is noteworthy that as he is one of the best quarterbacks, one of the qualities that could possibly make him so good is that he is not phased by away games as other football players might be. To see how another (not great) quarterback would compare to Brady‚Äôs performance, I ran the same analysis on Eli Manning, who had a lengthy, yet not as successful career. Thus, the higher Elo rating for away games may be due to two different reasons. First, quarterbacks may have to compensate for the challenges of an away game with better performance and secondly, the post game Elo ratings would increase a larger amount if they won a home versus away game, since the expectation of winning an away game is lower.
